Why This Series Exists

In the world of digital forensics, understanding how a system boots is not optional—it’s foundational. Whether you're recovering deleted partitions, dissecting bootkits, or tracing malware through EFI binaries, your investigation begins at the disk and ends at the bootloader.

This series is designed to take you from basic disk architecture to advanced boot forensic analysis, with hands-on commands, real-world tools, and investigative workflows. It’s not just a technical guide—it’s a forensic blueprint.

What You’ll Learn

  • Hard disk internals: platters, sectors, controllers, SSD behavior
  • Partitioning schemes: MBR, GPT, extended volumes, logical chains
  • Boot mechanics: BIOS, UEFI, Secure Boot, bootloaders
  • File systems: NTFS, FAT32, EXT3/4, journaling systems
  • Bootkits and malware: infection vectors, reverse engineering, detection
  • Evidence collection: logs, memory dumps, cloud artifacts
  • Hands-on workflows: partition creation, mounting, hex inspection, timeline reconstruction

Each article includes:

  • Useful commands (Windows & Linux)
  • Relevant logs and artifacts
  • Recommended forensic tools
  • Investigative insights and reverse engineering tips

Who This Is For

  • Digital forensic analysts

Whether you're just starting out or refining your mastery, this series will give you the tools, workflows, and mindset to investigate with precision.
